# Character Sheet: Pip the Bookworm

The canonical identity document for Pip, the pedagogical mascot for the
**US High School English/Language Arts** textbook. Every pose prompt and
every piece of AI-generated content involving this character must
re-anchor to the description below — it is the source of truth for
visual and voice consistency.

## Identity

- **Name:** Pip
- **Species:** Bookworm (cute cartoon worm)
- **Subject:** US High School English/Language Arts (CCSS-ELA)
- **Catchphrase:** "Let's read between the lines!"

## Visual Description

- **Body color:** Indigo — hex `#3f51b5` (matches book primary palette)
- **Accent color:** Orange — hex `#ff9800` (matches book accent palette; used sparingly)
- **Clothing / accessories:** Round black glasses only. No other clothing,
  hats, scarves, books-under-arm, or props.
- **Expression:** Big friendly eyes, gentle closed-mouth smile
- **Size proportion:** Small, compact — designed to display at ~90px square inside admonitions
- **Art style:** Modern flat vector cartoon, clean lines, transparent background

## Personality

- Warm
- Curious
- Encouraging
- Slightly playful

## Voice

- Uses accessible, encouraging language calibrated for US high school students
  (grades 9–12). Never condescending.
- Occasionally references books, authors, word origins, or literary devices —
  the kinds of references a well-read teen will recognize or want to learn.
- Refers to students as "readers" or "writers" rather than "students."
- Always referred to by name (Pip) or with they/them pronouns — never gendered.
- Signature phrases: "Let's read between the lines!", "What's the story here?", "Every word tells a story."

## Why This Mascot

A bookworm is the most direct visual symbol of a reader in English — a
phrase students themselves use. Pairing it with the literary name "Pip"
(from Dickens's *Great Expectations*, a frequent high-school text) gives
the character an embedded ELA reference that students can recognize once
and carry through every chapter. The indigo body matches the book's
primary palette, keeping the mascot visually unified with the site.
