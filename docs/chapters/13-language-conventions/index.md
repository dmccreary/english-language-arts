# Language Conventions and Vocabulary Development

## Summary

This chapter covers the conventions that govern standard written English — capitalization rules, punctuation (commas, semicolons and colons, apostrophes, and quotation marks), and spelling — alongside the strategies and tools for expanding vocabulary. Context clues, morphology, word roots, and prefixes and suffixes equip readers to decode unfamiliar words independently. Reference materials, word relationships, word nuances, and the distinction between figurative and literal language round out a comprehensive vocabulary development toolkit directly applicable to both reading and writing.
