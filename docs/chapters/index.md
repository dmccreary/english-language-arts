# Chapters

This textbook is organized into 17 chapters covering 295 concepts across the five CCSS-ELA strands — Reading Literature, Reading Informational Text, Writing, Speaking and Listening, and Language — plus cross-cutting skills in Critical Thinking, Media Literacy, Cognitive Bias, Systems Thinking, AI in Writing, and a senior Capstone.

## Chapter Overview

1. [Foundations of English Language Arts](01-foundations/index.md) — Introduces the ELA course framework, the five strands, CCR anchor standards, close reading, textual evidence, and vocabulary essentials.
2. [Literary Genres and Text Forms](02-literary-genres/index.md) — Surveys fiction, nonfiction, drama, poetry, and all major literary subgenres and forms.
3. [Narrative Elements: Plot, Character, and Point of View](03-narrative-elements/index.md) — Explores theme, central idea, character development, plot structure, conflict types, point of view, and author's choices.
4. [Figurative Language, Tone, and Author's Style](04-figurative-language/index.md) — Develops the full set of literary devices — metaphor to motif — and the dimensions of author's style, tone, and mood.
5. [Narrative Techniques, Literary Periods, and Comparative Analysis](05-narrative-techniques/index.md) — Covers foreshadowing, flashback, pacing, literary periods, Shakespeare, mythology, and comparing adaptations and mediums.
6. [Informational Text: Rhetoric, Argument, and Rhetorical Appeals](06-informational-text/index.md) — Introduces informational reading, summarizing, rhetoric, argument structure, claims, evidence, and the four rhetorical appeals.
7. [US Foundational Documents and Informational Sources](07-foundational-documents/index.md) — Examines seminal American documents and authors, scientific and technical texts, and multiple-source comparison.
8. [Critical Thinking, Logical Reasoning, and Fallacies](08-critical-thinking/index.md) — Builds critical and logical reasoning, argumentation skills, rhetorical analysis, and the seven named logical fallacies.
9. [The Writing Process, Essay Foundations, and Digital Tools](09-writing-process/index.md) — Walks through the full writing process, essay structure, task and audience awareness, and collaborative and digital writing.
10. [Writing Modes and Essay Development](10-writing-modes/index.md) — Develops argument, informative, explanatory, and narrative writing modes, thesis and body-paragraph craft, and expository patterns.
11. [Research, Citation, and Academic Integrity](11-research-citation/index.md) — Covers evidence integration, research writing, source credibility, plagiarism, MLA/APA citation, annotated bibliography, and research methodology.
12. [Standard English Grammar and Sentence Structure](12-grammar/index.md) — Teaches parts of speech, sentence types, phrases and clauses, and the full range of English sentence forms.
13. [Language Conventions and Vocabulary Development](13-language-conventions/index.md) — Addresses capitalization, punctuation, spelling, and vocabulary acquisition through context clues, morphology, and word relationships.
14. [Speaking, Listening, and Multimedia Presentation](14-speaking-listening/index.md) — Covers academic discussion, active listening, presentation and public speaking, digital media integration, and formal English.
15. [Media Literacy and Cognitive Bias](15-media-literacy/index.md) — Develops critical evaluation of media and sources and surveys eighteen cognitive biases that affect reading and reasoning.
16. [Systems Thinking and AI in Writing](16-systems-thinking-ai/index.md) — Introduces feedback loops, causal loop diagrams, and the responsible use of AI as a cowriting and brainstorming partner.
17. [Senior Capstone Project](17-capstone/index.md) — Synthesizes all prior learning into five capstone project options, including the senior research thesis, literary portfolio, and civic engagement portfolio.

## How to Use This Textbook

Chapters are sequenced so that each builds on concepts introduced earlier. Reading Literature (chapters 2–5) and Informational Text (chapters 6–7) precede the Writing strand (chapters 9–11) because writing skills depend on reading fluency and rhetorical awareness. Grammar and Language (chapters 12–13) are placed before Speaking (chapter 14) because formal English conventions underpin academic oral communication. Cross-cutting skills — Critical Thinking (chapter 8), Media Literacy and Cognitive Bias (chapter 15), and Systems Thinking and AI (chapter 16) — are distributed so that foundational literacy comes first and analytical tools are introduced once readers have substantive texts to apply them to.
