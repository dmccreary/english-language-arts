# References: The Writing Process, Essay Foundations, and Digital Tools

1. [Writing process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writing_process) - Wikipedia - Overview of the recursive writing process — prewriting, drafting, revising, editing, and publishing — including research on how writers actually work versus the linear model often taught in schools.

2. [Essay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Essay) - Wikipedia - History and definition of the essay as a literary form, coverage of its major structural types (personal, analytical, argumentative, expository), and its place in academic writing at all levels.

3. [Peer review](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peer_review) - Wikipedia - Explanation of peer review as a quality-control process, covering both academic publishing peer review and the classroom peer writing workshop model that this chapter introduces for collaborative revision.

4. The Bedford Handbook (11th ed.) - Diana Hacker and Nancy Sommers - Bedford/St. Martin's - The most widely adopted college-level writing handbook; its chapters on the writing process, essay structure, revision strategies, and digital writing tools map directly to this chapter's concept list.

5. Writing Arguments: A Rhetoric with Readings (10th ed.) - John Ramage, John Bean, and June Johnson - Pearson - Comprehensive writing textbook covering the recursive writing process, task and audience awareness, essay structure, and the use of peer workshop for revision — all core concepts in this chapter.

6. [Purdue OWL: The Writing Process](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/index.html) - Purdue Online Writing Lab - The most comprehensive free guide to all stages of the writing process including prewriting strategies, drafting, global revision, sentence-level editing, and publication — directly aligned with this chapter's concept list.

7. [National Writing Project](https://www.nwp.org) - National Writing Project - Teacher-led professional network with research-based resources on writing instruction; its writing process materials and peer workshop protocols support the collaborative writing and peer review skills in this chapter.

8. [ReadWriteThink](https://www.readwritethink.org) - National Council of Teachers of English / IRA - Free interactive graphic organizers (story maps, essay planners, Venn diagrams) and lesson plans supporting every stage of the writing process from prewriting through publishing.

9. [Khan Academy: Grammar and Writing](https://www.khanacademy.org/humanities/grammar) - Khan Academy - Free lessons on grammar, style, revision, and essay structure, with practice exercises supporting the editing and self-assessment skills this chapter develops.

10. [CommonLit](https://www.commonlit.org) - CommonLit - Free platform with writing prompts paired to CCSS reading passages, supporting the task, purpose, and audience awareness skills and the integration of reading and writing introduced in this chapter.
