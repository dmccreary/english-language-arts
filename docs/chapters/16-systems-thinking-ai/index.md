# Systems Thinking and AI in Writing

## Summary

This chapter covers two forward-looking analytical frameworks. The systems thinking section introduces feedback loops (balancing and reinforcing), causal loop diagrams, unintended consequences, holistic problem analysis, and second-order effects — equipping readers to analyze complex issues in texts and in the world. The AI in Writing section prepares readers to use large language models responsibly as cowriting partners: brainstorming, outlining, and soliciting feedback — while maintaining awareness of AI limitations (hallucination, bias, loss of voice), ethical and academic-integrity boundaries, AI disclosure and citation requirements, and the skills to critically evaluate AI-generated output.
