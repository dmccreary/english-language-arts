## Notes and Observations

- **Word count shortfalls were the most consistent challenge**: Nearly every chapter required 2–4 rounds of targeted expansion to reach 8,500 words. Expansion strategies used: adding worked examples, extending case studies, adding practical application sections, adding tables with prose support, and inserting bridging sections that connected concepts across the chapter.

- **Read-before-Write requirement**: Chapters that already had outline stubs required using the `Read` tool before `Write` would succeed. This was a consistent workflow requirement for Chapters 10–17.

- **Chapter 15 (26 concepts) and Chapter 16 (22 concepts)** were the largest concept lists and required the most structured organization. Chapter 15 was organized into two major sections (Media Literacy, Cognitive Bias) with the 18 biases grouped thematically into 4 clusters.

- **Chapter 17 (Capstone)** required the most iterative expansion due to the challenge of generating 8,500+ words from 9 relatively abstract concepts. The solution was detailed pathway descriptions, worked examples, a rubric, a checklist, a skills integration table, and a section on post-secondary application.

- **AI-generated content on AI (Chapter 16)**: Chapter 16's AI-in-writing section was written with particular care to be accurate, nuanced, and honest about AI limitations — including the limitations of AI-generated text itself.
