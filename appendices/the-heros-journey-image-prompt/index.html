    Appendix B — Joseph Campbell: Image Prompts¶

    Image prompts for illustrations used in the Joseph Campbell appendix. Each prompt is self-contained and can be used independently with any AI image generator. Suggested output size: 1536×1024 PNG (landscape) for in-chapter use, or 1024×1024 for square crop variants.

    1. The Hero's Journey Treasure Map¶

    Intended use¶

    Full-width illustration in the Hero's Journey section of Appendix B. File target: docs/img/appendices/heros-journey-treasure-map.png

    Prompt¶

    Create a richly detailed hand-illustrated treasure map showing the
seventeen stages of Joseph Campbell's Hero's Journey, organized into
three distinct phases: Departure, Initiation, and Return.

OVERALL STYLE
The map should look like a genuine antique treasure map — aged cream
parchment with slightly burned and water-stained edges, subtle foxing
spots, and the warm honey-gold tonality of old vellum. All illustration
elements are rendered in the style of a 17th–18th century nautical
cartographer: fine sepia-inked line work, gentle watercolor washes in
earth tones (ochre, sienna, sage green, dusty rose, deep teal), compass
roses, sea serpents in the margins, and small decorative scrollwork
borders. Lettering is hand-lettered in elegant copperplate calligraphy.
The overall feel should be warm, mysterious, and slightly fantastical —
inviting exploration rather than intimidating the viewer.

TITLE
At the very top center of the map, inside an ornate cartouche decorated
with laurel branches, an open book, and a winding worm motif, write in
large copperplate calligraphy:

    THE HERO'S JOURNEY

Below the title, in smaller italic lettering inside the same cartouche:

    After Joseph Campbell — "The Hero with a Thousand Faces" (1949)

COMPASS ROSE
Place a detailed compass rose in the upper-left corner showing all eight
cardinal and intercardinal directions. The North pointer is decorated
with a tiny flame, representing the call to adventure.

THE PATH
A single winding dotted trail — the kind seen on treasure maps — traces
the hero's journey across the entire map. The path begins at the
bottom-left corner (the Ordinary World) and winds, curves, and spirals
upward and rightward through increasingly dramatic terrain: gentle
rolling hills give way to dark forests, then stormy seas, then volcanic
mountains, then a descent into a cavern at the map's dark center, and
finally a winding return path back toward the bottom-right corner where
a treasure chest sits under a prominent red X. Small footprint icons
appear along the path between stages.

THREE PHASES — LABELED REGIONS
Divide the map into three visually distinct regions using faint
watercolor washes over the background parchment:

  • DEPARTURE — left third of the map: a warm golden-amber wash over
    gentle pastoral landscape. A small banner label reads DEPARTURE in
    red ink, placed in the upper portion of this region.

  • INITIATION — center and upper-center of the map: a stormy dark-
    teal wash over a landscape of mountains, forests, deep caverns, and
    churning seas. A small banner label reads INITIATION in deep crimson
    ink.

  • RETURN — right third of the map: a warm sunrise-gold wash with
    clearing skies and the path descending back toward home. A small
    banner label reads RETURN in green ink.

THE SEVENTEEN STAGES — SPECIFIC ILLUSTRATIONS
Place each stage as a small labeled vignette alongside the path. Each
vignette is roughly the size of a postage stamp — a tiny, detailed pen-
and-ink sketch with a calligraphic stage number and name below it.

Stage 1 — THE ORDINARY WORLD (bottom-left)
  Sketch: A cozy thatched-roof cottage with smoke curling from the
  chimney, surrounded by a vegetable garden and a wooden fence. A
  young figure stands at the door, looking out. The surrounding
  landscape is flat and peaceful.

Stage 2 — THE CALL TO ADVENTURE (just above Stage 1, path beginning)
  Sketch: A mysterious herald figure on horseback, bearing a glowing
  scroll or a lantern. The young hero watches from a distance, hand
  raised to shield their eyes. Above the herald, a shooting star.

Stage 3 — REFUSAL OF THE CALL (nearby on path)
  Sketch: The hero standing at a fork in the road, arms crossed, back
  turned to the path ahead. Their shadow stretches toward the adventure.
  A raven perches on the signpost, watching.

Stage 4 — SUPERNATURAL AID (on the path, approaching the first gate)
  Sketch: A wise, robed mentor figure with a long staff — gender
  ambiguous, ancient-looking — gesturing encouragingly. Between them,
  a softly glowing object: a sword, a book, or a talisman, rendered
  with a faint radiant halo of gold ink.

Stage 5 — CROSSING THE FIRST THRESHOLD (a stone archway spanning the path)
  Sketch: An imposing stone arch, carved with mythological beasts —
  a lion on one side, a serpent on the other. The hero passes through,
  their silhouette small against the dark opening. On the far side of
  the arch, the landscape changes from golden to shadowed.

Stage 6 — THE ROAD OF TRIALS (winding through the Initiation zone)
  Sketch: Three small sequential vignettes along the path:
    (a) a labyrinth entrance with a minotaur's shadow inside
    (b) a rickety bridge over a chasm with a riddle-sphinx crouching at
        the far end
    (c) a stormy sea with a small boat and a serpent rising from the
        waves
  Label the cluster: ROAD OF TRIALS.

Stage 7 — MEETING WITH THE GODDESS (a clearing in a dark forest)
  Sketch: A luminous, ethereal figure — a goddess, radiant with calm
  authority — standing in a beam of moonlight that breaks through the
  tree canopy. She holds a torch in one hand and a mirror in the other.
  The hero kneels before her in awe.

Stage 8 — WOMAN AS TEMPTRESS / INNER TEMPTATION (a rocky shoreline)
  Sketch: A beautiful but dangerous coastline with sirens on rocks in
  the distance, their song rendered as curling musical-note waves
  drifting toward the hero's ship. The hero is lashed to the mast,
  hands covering their ears, eyes squeezed shut.

Stage 9 — ATONEMENT WITH THE FATHER (at the base of a great mountain)
  Sketch: Two figures facing each other — the hero and a towering
  father-authority figure in armor or robes — at the foot of a mountain
  whose peak is hidden in storm clouds. Lightning illuminates the scene.
  They are not fighting; they are seeing each other, perhaps for the
  first time.

Stage 10 — APOTHEOSIS (near the mountaintop, a moment of transcendence)
  Sketch: The hero at the mountain's summit, arms outstretched, body
  surrounded by a radiant solar corona. The figure appears
  simultaneously small (against the vast sky) and immense (glowing with
  inner light). Stars visible even in daylight.

Stage 11 — THE ULTIMATE BOON (at the map's dark center — the inmost cave)
  Sketch: Deep inside a cavern whose entrance is marked by a skull-
  and-bones warning symbol (in the treasure-map tradition), the hero
  holds up the boon — a blazing golden chalice, a luminous orb, or an
  open book radiating light. The cave walls glitter with reflected
  gold. This is the most elaborate single vignette on the map.

Stage 12 — REFUSAL OF THE RETURN (a tempting paradise island nearby)
  Sketch: A lush island with palm trees, a warm fire, abundant food —
  the hero sitting contentedly, while behind them the path home is
  clearly visible. A small ghost-like image of their community appears
  in the sky like a fading vision, reminding them of obligations.

Stage 13 — THE MAGIC FLIGHT (the path curves sharply back toward home)
  Sketch: The hero in full sprint or riding a magical creature —
  a winged horse, a great eagle — the boon clutched to their chest,
  pursued by shadowy figures or storm clouds that cannot quite catch
  them. A sense of speed and barely-controlled exhilaration.

Stage 14 — RESCUE FROM WITHOUT (a helping hand extended from off-path)
  Sketch: A figure reaching down from above — perhaps from a rope
  ladder, a ship's railing, or a cloud — extending a strong arm to the
  hero below, who reaches up. The rescue is communal: it cannot be
  completed alone.

Stage 15 — CROSSING THE RETURN THRESHOLD (the same stone arch as Stage 5)
  Sketch: The same arch from Stage 5, now seen from the other side.
  The hero passes back through it, older, changed, carrying the boon.
  The beasts carved on the arch now seem to bow slightly. The landscape
  ahead is golden again.

Stage 16 — MASTER OF TWO WORLDS (on the border between dark and light zones)
  Sketch: The hero standing at the exact border between shadow and
  sunlight on the map, one foot in each zone. They look simultaneously
  comfortable in both — not torn, but integrated. A halo of both gold
  and silver surrounds them.

Stage 17 — FREEDOM TO LIVE (bottom-right, near the treasure chest)
  Sketch: The hero back in a village — similar to the cottage in
  Stage 1, but now richer and more alive with community. The hero is
  surrounded by people receiving the boon. Children listen to a story
  being told. Lights glow in every window. A great sense of peace.

THE TREASURE CHEST (bottom-right corner — the map's end)
Draw a large, ornately detailed wooden treasure chest bound with iron
straps, sitting on the parchment with a dramatic drop shadow. The chest
is half-open, spilling golden coins, glowing books, a chalice, and a
small worm figurine (a nod to the ELA mascot Pip). Above the chest, a
large red X drawn in broad brushstroke ink. A curling banner beside the
chest reads in calligraphy:

    "Follow your bliss."
    — Joseph Campbell

MARGINAL DECORATIONS (around the edges of the parchment)
In the tradition of antique cartography, fill the sea and empty-margin
areas with:
  • Two large sea serpents in the oceans, labeled HĪC SVNT DRACONES
    ("Here be dragons")
  • A small galleon under full sail in the upper-right ocean area
  • A cartouche in the lower-left corner showing a scale bar labeled
    "One Life's Journey"
  • Small decorative vignettes: an open book, a quill pen, a lyre, a
    mask (comedy/tragedy), and a single burning candle — all traditional
    symbols of storytelling
  • In the very lower-right margin, a tiny colophon-style credit line:
    "Cartographed after the work of Joseph Campbell, 1904–1987"

COLOR PALETTE
  Parchment base:     warm cream #F5E6C8 with aging variations
  Ink lines:          deep sepia #3D2B1F
  Departure wash:     warm ochre #D4A017 at 25% opacity
  Initiation wash:    storm teal #2C5F6B at 20% opacity
  Return wash:        dawn gold #E8C547 at 20% opacity
  Path dotted line:   rich sienna #8B3A2A
  Stage labels:       deep navy #1A2744
  Phase banners:      Departure red #8B1A1A, Initiation crimson #6B0000,
                      Return green #2D5A27
  Treasure X:         bold crimson #CC0000
  Gold accents:       #C5A028

OUTPUT SPECIFICATIONS
  • Aspect ratio: 3:2 landscape (e.g. 1536×1024 px)
  • No white background — use the parchment texture as the only
    background
  • All text must be legible at full resolution
  • No photographic or realistic elements — hand-illustrated map style
    throughout
  • The overall impression should be: beautiful, navigable, and rich
    with storytelling detail — a map a student would want to pin on
    their wall

    Variation: Square Crop for Chapter Header¶

    [Use the same prompt as above, but request 1024×1024 square output
and ask the generator to crop to the central section of the map —
Stages 5 through 14 — from the First Threshold through the Supreme
Ordeal and beginning of the Return. Preserve the title cartouche
at the top and the phase labels DEPARTURE / INITIATION / RETURN.
The treasure chest with the X should be visible but at the right
edge, drawing the eye forward.]

    Notes for Art Direction¶

    • If the AI generator cannot render legible hand-lettered text, request the illustration without text labels and overlay the stage names using the site's CSS (positioned absolutely over an <img> tag in a <div class="map-container">).
    • The sea serpent marginal decorations may be omitted if the generator renders them too prominently and they distract from the path.
    • The Pip mascot Easter egg (small worm figurine in the treasure chest) is optional — include it if the generator handles small details cleanly.
